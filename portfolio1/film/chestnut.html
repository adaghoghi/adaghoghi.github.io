chestnut drive is about a married couple who spend the weekend at the husband's father's house.
it's the first time the wife has ever met her husband's father.

i was tasked with making a cross. it came out to be around four feet tall. sourced materials from home depot.
on set i was tasked with carving out the name of a character on it.
prior to this, i haven't had much experience constructing things with my hands.
i've since gained an appreciation and desire to continue doing this work for decor or utility purposes.

i made a 'pet portrait' of the father's favorite dog, who had passed away, named 'Al'.
i had never made anything before this point that was intended to be printed.
it was a fun learning process figuring out where was the best location to get it printed,
what kind of print was necessary,
and making sure everything was sized and format correctly.
