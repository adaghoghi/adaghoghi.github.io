ASSIGNMENT 3



I need to have more of a perspective from the pro-football side of things.
That’s why I wanted to watch this TED talk from Angus Reid, Why We Need High School Football.
He mentions that he is explicitly in favor of high school football, not youth, or professional.
Football, especially high school football, in his words, allows you to learn to set goals for yourself, how to win and how to lose, how to handle pressure, summarizing into, ‘qualities that make you a productive adult.’

A major point for him is that football gives structure in the lives of those playing it.
You know you have one game a week in the six-month season, and that you have practice every day after-school leading up to that game.
For kids who may not come from a stable home, this is a great way of gaining structure in their lives.
Teaching them that it is possible to have a structured life.

He also mentions that, ‘football is the only sport that doesn’t exclude anyone.’
High school teams want numbers, and they openly ask students to come and try out.
There is also no barrier to entry, schools support the sport so financial costs can be minimal compared to other sports, and athletic ability isn’t as big of an issue in high school football.

While I don’t necessarily have the resources to dispute his claims, he went to high school in Canada.
I have a feeling that if he were to go to high school, in let’s say, Texas.
He would be having a little bit of a different opinion.
Parents do spend a lot of money in football, a lot of gear and training, nutrition, is all required at the top level.

However, his point overall was that you don’t need to or even desire to have a career in football to gain a lot out of it.
There, I can agree with him, however I believe that the dangers of the sport are just much too risky to go into the sport.
There are other avenues to explore as a high school student that can give you structure.
Clubs? Extracurriculars? Relationships with your teachers? More brainstorm is required, but I think he brings up a good point.
